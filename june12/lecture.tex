\section{some case functions}

f(x) = 1 for some x in q and 0 for ome x in Q 
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } x \in \mathbb{Q} \\ 0 & \text{if } x \not \in \mathbb{Q} \end{cases}$$

is it continous? 
prove $f$ not continous anywhere 
\begin{proof}
    discontinuous at x if $\exists \epsilon > 0$ such that $\forall \delta > 0$ there exists $y$ such that $|x-y| < \delta$ and $|f(x) - f(y)| \geq \epsilon$
\end{proof}
